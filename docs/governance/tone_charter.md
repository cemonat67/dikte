# Operational Tone Charter

## The Communication Constitution

This document guarantees the psychological safety and executive calmness of the Zero@Trust platform. Any future development, copywriting, or feature addition MUST adhere to these laws. If a new feature requires communication that violates this charter, the feature must be redesigned. The tone is immovable.

### The Philosophy
Zero@Trust speaks like a seasoned board member or a stoic COO. It does not panic, it does not exaggerate, and it does not attempt to impress the user with technical jargon. It states facts and awaits instruction.

### The Prohibited Lexicon (ASLA)
Under no circumstances shall the platform use the following terms or their equivalents:
- ❌ Panic / Warning / Alert / Danger
- ❌ Urgent / Immediate / Critical
- ❌ Attack / Breach / Hack / Compromise
- ❌ AI Generated / Smart Insight / Magic
- ❌ Threat Level / Vulnerability Detected

### The Mandated Lexicon (HER ZAMAN)
The platform must strictly embody the following atmospheric tones:
- ✅ **Restrained:** Less is always more. Omit needless words.
- ✅ **Observational:** Describe what changed, not what it "means" dramatically.
- ✅ **Governance-Oriented:** Position the human as the final authority.
- ✅ **Human-Readable:** Avoid hex codes, raw IP addresses, and stack traces in the primary UI.
- ✅ **Operational:** Focus on the business impact (continuity), not the technical mechanism.
- ✅ **Calm:** Emit confidence through silence.

### Governance Prompts
Instead of demanding action, the system offers pathways:
- *Bad:* "Fix this issue immediately!"
- *Good:* "Review advised."

- *Bad:* "Block user now."
- *Good:* "Maintain constraint."

### The Rule of Silence
If there is nothing meaningful to say, say nothing. "Zero@Trust" avoids notification addiction. The absence of alerts is not a void to be filled with trivial data; it is the ultimate expression of trust.
